Sequential Dog-Ring Gearbox

Overview

Project Summary

A concise summary of the project's goals, scope, and my role in developing this high-performance gearbox concept.

This project involved the design and development of a Custom Sequential Dog-Ring Gearbox optimized for a high-performance Two-Stroke engine. The primary goal was to manage high torsional stress while achieving maximum transmission efficiency and ultra-fast, reliable shifting. My role covered the complete engineering process, from kinematic analysis and gear ratio definition to comprehensive CAD modeling of the shafts, the straight-cut gear train, and the shift mechanism. A critical aspect was the material selection (Case-Hardening Steels) and the fine-tuning of the dog-ring geometry to ensure positive engagement and prevent gear jump-out under the 2T engine's violent torque pulses. The final design successfully delivered a transmission system with superior mechanical holding power under load and exceptional service life, critical for maximizing the high-power output of the engine.

Process

Design & Engineering

A step-by-step narrative of the project journey, from initial concept to simulation.

1. Concept & CAD Modeling

The process began with in-depth research into modern sequential gearbox architectures, particularly those used in high-performance racing applications. Initial sketches were transformed into detailed 3D models using SolidWorks, focusing specifically on compact packaging, optimal shift fork kinematics, and structural integrity. Every critical component, from the gear teeth and dog rings to the main shafts and shift drum, was meticulously modeled.

2. Simulation & Analysis

Simulations of gear and shaft behavior under high load conditions, as well as critical analyses like tooth strength (Hertzian contact calculations) and transmission efficiency, were conducted using MATLAB. These simulations were crucial for optimizing the final gear ratios, ensuring reliable shift engagement kinetics, and verifying the overall structural durability under various operating extremes before any physical prototyping commenced.

Problem Solving

Challenges & Solutions

Primary technical hurdles encountered and the innovative solutions developed to overcome them.

Maximizing Transmission Efficiency and Durability expand_more

Solution: A low-friction surface finish (such as lapping or DLC - Diamond-Like Carbon coatings) was applied to the high-stress gears and dog rings. This coating reduces sliding and rolling friction, increases wear resistance, and significantly improves heat dissipation. This optimization was combined with a fine-tuning of the gear tooth profile to minimize power losses during torque transfer.

Reducing Friction and Mechanical Reliability expand_more

Solution: To support the shafts, precision ball bearings with silver or polymer cages (for high-slipperiness) were selected to maximize the speed limit and seizure resistance. Furthermore, an optimized lubrication system (separate oil bath or spray jet system) was engineered, and main bearings with increased clearances (C4/C5) were utilized to manage thermal expansion and high rotational speeds, ensuring long-term durability.
