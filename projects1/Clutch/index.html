6-Spring Multi-Disc Clutch

Overview

Project Summary

A concise summary of the project's goals, scope, and my role in developing this high-performance clutch concept.

This project showcases the design and development of the custom 6-Spring Multi-Disc Clutch for the two-stroke engine, focusing on managing high torque and achieving maximum performance through materials engineering and mechanical optimization. My role encompassed the entire process—from initial concept and CAD modeling (for the hub and basket) to the targeted selection of lightweight alloys for reduced rotating mass and dynamic validation of the disc pack performance. The main objective was to engineer a clutch system that ensured fast and reliable engagement, providing maximum holding power and exceptional thermal dissipation, while maintaining mechanical reliability and efficiency under the extreme operating conditions typical of high-performance engines.

Process

Design & Engineering

A step-by-step narrative of the project journey, from initial concept to simulation.

1. Concept & CAD Modeling

The process began with extensive research into the multi-plate clutch architectures commonly found in modern motorcycles, focusing on torque management and thermal dissipation. The initial sketches were transformed into detailed 3D models using SolidWorks. Each clutch assembly component –including basket, hub, friction plates, and steel plates– has been meticulously molded, emphasizing compactness and structural integrity to ensure precise engagement, excellent performance, and extended life.

2. Simulation & Analysis

Simulations were conducted to analyze the interaction of the clutch components, its torque transmission capabilities, and thermal behavior. Utilizing tools such as MATLAB, the dynamic engagement response, specific pressure on the plates, and endurance under load were thoroughly assessed across various operating conditions. These simulations were crucial for optimizing material selection, the number and geometry of the plates, and spring forces, thereby ensuring maximum reliability prior to any physical prototyping.

Problem Solving

Challenges & Solutions

Primary technical hurdles encountered and the innovative solutions developed to overcome them.

Reducing Mechanical Friction and Enhancing Reliability expand_more

Solution: The gearbox and clutch were engineered to utilize a separate oil bath, a crucial choice for optimally managing thermal issues and contamination distinct from the main engine oil. Within the clutch itself, this approach involved employing high-precision friction and steel discs with an optimized geometry to ensure rapid and clean disengagement, thereby minimizing drag when the clutch is open. Furthermore, advanced materials were selected for the friction surfaces, capable of withstanding extreme shear forces and intense thermal cycling without succumbing to wear or performance fade. The design of the basket and hub (as seen in the CAD model) carefully addressed tolerances and surface finishes to maximize component lifespan and minimize play or rattling, ensuring long-term durability even under maximum stress

Maximizing Torque Capacity and Durability (HP/L) expand_more

Solution: To safely handle the engine's maximum torque, the clutch pack design was optimized by precisely calculating the number of friction plates and the necessary spring pre-load. This was achieved through the selection of precision springs with a specifically tailored rate and length to provide the exact required clamping force, while simultaneously avoiding excessive effort at the clutch lever. Finally, the clutch benefits from integrated thermal management—thanks to the dedicated oil bath—which is fundamental for dissipating the heat generated during slipping and engagement, preserving the integrity of the discs and the oil film during maximum power transmission.
